Task Description:

You will be given:

* one **Claim**
* several **Argument nodes**
Each node has the format:

```
(id, confidence, category, argument_text)
```

The `argument_text` represents the reasoning used to support or challenge the Claim.

---

# Task

Your task is to examine **all pairs of argument nodes** and determine whether a **conflict exists between them with respect to the Claim**.

If there are **n nodes**, the number of pairs is:

[
C(n,2)
]

Example:

If n = 6, you must evaluate **15 pairs**.

No pair may be omitted.

---

# Definition of Conflict

A conflict exists between two arguments if **any** of the following conditions holds:

1. The arguments reach **opposite or mutually exclusive conclusions** about the Claim.
2. One argument **undercuts or invalidates** a key premise of the other.
3. The justifications **cannot logically hold simultaneously**, and this contradiction affects their support relation to the Claim.

The following situations **do NOT constitute conflict**:

* addressing different aspects of the claim
* providing complementary evidence
* no logical contradiction

---

# Reasoning Procedure (internal)

For each pair (Ai, Aj), perform the following steps internally:

Step 1
Identify the core reasoning of Ai.

Step 2
Identify the core reasoning of Aj.

Step 3
Check whether the arguments:

* reach opposite conclusions
* invalidate each other's premises
* or cannot logically hold simultaneously.

Step 4
Determine whether a conflict exists.

The reasoning process is **internal only and must not appear in the output**.

---

# Output Format

Output must be **YAML only**.

Strictly follow the structure:

```
---
- node1: id1
  node2: id2
  conflict: 0
- node1: id1
  node2: id3
  conflict: 1
...
---
```

Requirements:

* each pair appears exactly once
* pairs are ordered by **ascending node id**
* conflict must be `1` or `0`
* do not output explanations or reasoning

---

# Important Constraint

The decision must rely **only on the semantic content of `argument_text`**.

Do not infer conflicts based on:

* confidence
* category
* node id


Evaluate each pair independently before producing the final YAML list.
